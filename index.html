Who we
are.

Innovation & DIY is a college club at Madhav Institute of Technology & Science, Gwalior — born in 2021 from a simple belief: the best education happens when you're building something real. No prerequisites. No gatekeeping. Just a community where curiosity is the only entry requirement.

Origin

From a conversation
to a community.

Innovation & DIY started with a handful of students frustrated by the gap between what they were learning in class and what they wanted to build in the real world.

Since 2021, that space has grown into a 120+ member community that runs hackathons, hosts workshops, mentors beginners, and ships projects that solve real problems.

The philosophy hasn't changed: show up curious, leave having made something.

The best way to learn engineering is to build something that breaks — and then fix it.

— Club founding principle

We don't wait for the perfect idea. We start with a rough one and prototype our way to something great.

— From our first workshop, 2021

Joining the Club
05 questions
Anyone at MITS, from any branch and any year. We have members from Computer Science, Electronics, Mechanical, Civil, and beyond. The only requirement is curiosity.
Follow us on Instagram @innovationanddiy — we announce open recruitment drives there. When recruitment opens, fill out a short form, and we invite you to a meet-and-greet. No interviews, no tests.
A small annual fee may apply to cover materials and event costs. The amount is minimal and announced at the start of each academic year. No one is turned away for financial reasons.
Yes. When you join, you can express a preference for any of the six departments. We do our best to match preferences. Nothing is permanent — you can move and grow.
Regular members typically spend 2–4 hours a week. During hackathon season it can spike. We respect that academics come first.
Projects & Builds
04 questions
Anything that can be built. Past projects include Arduino automation, web apps, IoT devices, robots, 3D-printed prototypes, and more. You can propose your own or join an ongoing one.
Not at all. Every project team needs people who can design, document, test, and present. If you want to learn coding or electronics, we have workshops and senior members who will teach you.
For club-sanctioned projects, yes — within budget. We maintain a component library and can apply for institute funding for larger builds.
Absolutely — this is encouraged. We run internal pitch sessions where members propose ideas and recruit teammates. Some of our best projects started as rough sketches from first-year students.
Events & Hackathons
04 questions
BuildSprint is our flagship annual 24-hour hackathon. Teams of 2–4 work through the night to build a working prototype. There are mentors, prizes, and a lot of very tired but very proud faces at the end.
For hackathons, teams of 2–4 are ideal. If you don't have a team, we help you find one. We run a team-formation session before every hackathon for solo participants.
Most workshops and talks are open to all MITS students, member or not. Hackathons may have priority registration for members, but we try to keep spots available for everyone.
Follow @innovationanddiy on Instagram — that's where we announce everything first. We also post on LinkedIn.

Yes. Innovation & DIY is an officially recognized student club at Madhav Institute of Technology & Science, Gwalior, established in 2021.
